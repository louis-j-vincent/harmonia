How Semi-Markov Fixes Phase 1 Fragmentation

The Problem: Before semi-Markov, root-change segmentation produced 1.12–2.0 chords/bar because:

  • A single beat with wrong-root acoustic activation (bass playing the 5th) would create a spurious 1-beat segment
  • That segment's summed root posterior, even over 1 beat, might favor the wrong root
  • Grammar constraints (ii-V-I) can't fix audio that whispers wrong

The Solution: Semi-Markov Viterbi with duration prior:

  • For each candidate duration d (1–32 beats), score = beat_emission[t..t+d] + log P(d) + transition
  • The duration prior P(d) is the Bayesian weight against short d values
  • With dur_weight=0.25, P(1-beat)≈5% makes 1-beat segments expensive (not impossible)
  • The decoder merges that stray beat into its 2- or 4-beat neighbors, whose summed root posterior is stable

What it Does NOT Fix:

  • Systematic bass-5th confusion over 2+ bars (still acoustic)
  • Quality classification (re-done by the strong segment classifier)
  • Bar-boundary phase recovery (depends on downbeat alignment)
