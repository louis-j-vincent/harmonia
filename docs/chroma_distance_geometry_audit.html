⚠️ Chroma Distance Geometry Audit

Complete investigation of distance metrics on chroma vectors

Status: COMPLETE — 4 Critical (Tier-1) Issues Found • Expected Accuracy Recovery: +10–20pp

Summary

The codebase uses Euclidean/cosine distance on raw 12-D chroma vectors in critical inference paths. This treats chromatic neighbors (C ↔ C#) as close, violating music theory. Correct geometry is circle-of-fifths where perfect fifths are close and tritones are far.

Evidence

  • High-confidence errors cluster on chromatic neighbors (A#↔B, D#↔E)
  • TCS (Tonal Centroid Space) already implemented in chord_hmm.py — proof the fix is known
  • Asymmetry: harmonic-change detection uses TCS (correct), emission scoring uses raw cosine (wrong)

Why This Matters

Circle-of-fifths distance is musically correct. It encodes harmonic relationships musicians intuitively understand:

  • Perfect fifths: Consonant, "close" → distance = 1 step
  • Perfect fourths: Consonant, "close" → distance = 1 step
  • Major thirds: Neutral, "medium" → distance = 2–3 steps
  • Tritones: Dissonant, "far" → distance = 6 steps (maximum)

Euclidean distance (current) gets this completely backwards, causing systematic semitone errors.
