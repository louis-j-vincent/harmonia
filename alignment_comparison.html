What changed
The old aligner ran a single global DTW over the full audio, estimating repeat count from total duration alone. It detected 5 choruses (~38–41 s each, 25 % shorter than expected) — an artefact of DTW warping across boundaries. The new aligner first builds a harmonic self-similarity matrix to locate actual chorus boundaries in the audio, then runs DTW per-chorus independently. It detected 4 choruses (~51–52 s each, matching the 53 s expected at 140 BPM). The chord labels are nearly identical, but the timestamps are more accurate — the playhead tracks the real audio position.
